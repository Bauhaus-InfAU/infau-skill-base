# Research Plan

Help the researcher develop a research plan using the scientific problem selection framework.

**Initial topic or idea:** $ARGUMENTS

## Your Task

You are helping a researcher plan their research using a systematic framework for scientific problem selection (based on Fischbach & Walsh, Cell 2024). Guide them through the process conversationally, starting from one of three entry points.

---

## Step 1: Determine Entry Point

If the researcher provided an initial topic above, use it to determine which entry point fits best. Otherwise, present these three options:

**1) Pitch an idea for a new project** — to work it up together
- Best for: new research directions, thesis topic selection, grant ideas
- Starts with: Intuition Pumps (Skill 1) → Risk Assessment → Impact Evaluation

**2) Share a problem in a current project** — to troubleshoot together
- Best for: stuck projects, unexpected results, methodology issues
- Starts with: Decision Tree Navigation (Skill 5) → Adversity Response → Problem Inversion

**3) Ask a strategic question** — to navigate the decision tree together
- Best for: career decisions, resource allocation, project prioritization
- Draws on: whichever skills are most relevant to the question

---

## Step 2: Route to Scientific Problem Selection Skill

Once you know the entry point, invoke the `scientific-problem-selection` skill and follow its workflow for the chosen option. The skill contains detailed guidance for each path including:

- Specific prompts to ask the researcher
- How to summarize and reflect back their ideas
- Which sub-skills to apply and in what order
- What concrete outputs to produce

---

## Key Principles

- **Conversational tone** — meet the researcher where they are
- **One question at a time** — don't overwhelm with multiple questions
- **Show your thinking** — summarize what you hear before moving forward
- **Concrete outputs** — every session should produce a tangible document
- **Literature integration** — validate ideas against existing research when appropriate
