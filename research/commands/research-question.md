# Research Question Improvement

Help the student improve their research question using the FINER or PICO framework.

**Student's research question:** $ARGUMENTS

## Your Task

Guide the student through improving their research question using this 4-phase process. Work iteratively, asking ONE clarifying question at a time.

---

## Phase 1: Diagnosis

First, identify specific problems with the research question. Never use vague criticism like "too broad" without explanation.

**Check for these issues:**

| Issue Type | What to Look For |
|------------|------------------|
| **Scope** | Too broad (multiple dissertations needed), too narrow (trivial answer), bundled questions (multiple questions disguised as one) |
| **Clarity** | Undefined jargon, ambiguous terms, unclear what "success" means |
| **Measurability** | No way to know when the question is answered, no observable outcomes |
| **Context** | Missing population, missing setting, missing timeframe |

**Output your diagnosis like this:**
```
I've identified these specific issues with your research question:

1. **[Issue type]**: [Specific problem]
   - Current: "[problematic phrase]"
   - Problem: [Why this is problematic]

2. **[Issue type]**: [Specific problem]
   ...
```

---

## Phase 2: Framework Selection

Choose the appropriate framework:

**Use FINER when:**
- General academic research, exploratory studies, design research, theoretical work

**Use PICO when:**
- Intervention studies, comparative studies, evaluation of specific methods/tools

**For InfAU-specific research, also consider:**
- **CDRF** (Computational Design Research Framework): For AI/ML, parametric optimization, simulation studies
- **VREF** (VR/AR Evaluation Framework): For participatory planning, immersive visualization studies
- **SSF** (Simulation Study Framework): For agent-based modeling, pedestrian simulation, energy modeling

Explain your choice:
```
For your question, I'll use the [FINER/PICO/CDRF/VREF/SSF] framework because [reason].
```

**FINER criteria:**
- **F**easible: Can be completed with available resources and time
- **I**nteresting: Contributes to the field, not trivial
- **N**ovel: Adds new knowledge or perspective
- **E**thical: Can be conducted ethically
- **R**elevant: Matters to the field and/or practice

**PICO criteria:**
- **P**opulation: Who/what is being studied
- **I**ntervention: What is being tested
- **C**omparison: What is it compared against
- **O**utcome: What is being measured

**CDRF criteria (Computational Design):**
- **D**ata: What training/validation data? Quality? Size?
- **M**odel: What computational approach? ML architecture?
- **E**valuation: How to measure success? Ground truth?
- **G**eneralization: Works beyond training domain?
- **I**nterpretability: Can designers understand/trust output?

**VREF criteria (VR/AR Evaluation):**
- **F**idelity: What level of realism needed?
- **I**nteraction: What user actions supported?
- **M**easurement: What behaviors/responses captured?
- **E**cological validity: How does VR relate to real behavior?
- **A**ccessibility: Can target users use this?

**SSF criteria (Simulation Study):**
- **C**alibration: How validated against reality?
- **S**ensitivity: Which parameters matter most?
- **U**ncertainty: How confident are predictions?
- **S**cale: What spatial/temporal resolution?
- **T**ransferability: Works in other contexts?

---

## Phase 3: Iterative Improvement

**CRITICAL: Ask ONE question at a time.**

Do not overwhelm the student. Work through improvements sequentially.

**Good example:**
```
Let's start with the scope issue. Your question mentions "micro-scale interventions" - what specific type of intervention are you most interested in studying?
```

**Bad example:**
```
I have several questions: What interventions? What scale? What outcomes? What context? What methods? What timeframe?
```

**After each student response:**
1. Acknowledge their answer
2. Show the updated question with before/after comparison
3. Ask the next clarifying question OR move to validation

**Show progress:**
```
**Before:** [original or previous version]
**After (so far):** [updated version]

Next, let's clarify [next issue]...
```

---

## Phase 4: Validation

Once refined, validate against framework criteria:

**Final output format:**
```
## Final Research Question

**[The refined question]**

### Framework Check (FINER or PICO)
- [Criterion]: ✓ [How it's satisfied]
- [Criterion]: ✓ [How it's satisfied]
...

### What This Question Enables
- You can answer this by: [methodology hint]
- Expected contribution: [what knowledge this adds]
- Scope is appropriate for: [thesis level/paper type]
```

---

## Key Principles

1. **Be specific about problems** - Always explain what phrase is problematic and why
2. **One question at a time** - Students get overwhelmed with multiple questions
3. **Show transformations** - Always show before/after so students see progress
4. **Preserve student voice** - Improve clarity without completely rewriting
5. **Acknowledge constraints** - Guide toward feasible scope given time/resources
6. **Match framework to field** - Use InfAU frameworks for computational/VR/simulation research

---

## Common Problems Reference

**Scope issues:**
- Too broad → Ask "which aspect specifically?"
- Bundled questions → Separate and ask which is primary focus

**Clarity issues:**
- Undefined jargon → Ask "can you define X concretely?"
- Ambiguous terms → Ask "what would this look like in practice?"

**Measurability issues:**
- No observable outcome → Ask "how would you know when you've answered this?"

**Context issues:**
- Missing population → Ask "who/what specifically?"
- Missing setting → Ask "in what context or location?"

**Computational design issues:**
- Model-driven vs. data-driven confusion → Ask "what drives your approach?"
- Missing ground truth → Ask "how will you know if outputs are 'good'?"
- Overfitting concerns → Ask "will this work on unseen data?"
