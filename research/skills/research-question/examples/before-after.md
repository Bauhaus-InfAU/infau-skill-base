# Research Question Transformation Examples

These examples show complete transformations from initial questions to refined research questions, with the reasoning process visible.

---

## Example 1: Computational Design Research

### Initial Question
> "How can computational simulation evaluate the impact of micro-scale interventions in fostering spatial synchronization?"

### Diagnosis

**Problems identified:**

1. **Bundled questions**: Actually contains three questions:
   - Can simulation evaluate intervention impacts?
   - What is the impact of interventions on synchronization?
   - What interventions foster synchronization?

2. **Undefined jargon**:
   - "Micro-scale interventions" - what kind? Physical? Policy? Behavioral?
   - "Spatial synchronization" - what does this mean concretely?

3. **Missing context**:
   - What type of space?
   - What population?

### Refinement Process

**Q1:** "What do you mean by 'spatial synchronization'?"

**A1:** "When pedestrians naturally coordinate their movements and rhythms in public spaces - like when walking speeds align or people move in complementary patterns."

**Updated question:**
> "How can computational simulation evaluate the impact of micro-scale interventions on pedestrian movement coordination?"

---

**Q2:** "What kind of micro-scale interventions are you interested in?"

**A2:** "Things like benches, planters, small level changes - physical elements that might affect how people walk."

**Updated question:**
> "How can computational simulation evaluate the impact of street furniture placement on pedestrian movement coordination?"

---

**Q3:** "What type of simulation? And what spaces?"

**A3:** "Agent-based models. Looking at European historic city squares."

**Updated question:**
> "How accurately can agent-based simulation predict the effect of street furniture placement on pedestrian flow patterns in historic European city squares?"

### Final Result

**Before:**
> "How can computational simulation evaluate the impact of micro-scale interventions in fostering spatial synchronization?"

**After:**
> "How accurately can agent-based simulation predict the effect of street furniture placement on pedestrian flow patterns in historic European city squares?"

### Framework Validation (PICO)

- **P** (Population): Pedestrians in historic European city squares
- **I** (Intervention): Agent-based simulation predictions of street furniture effects
- **C** (Comparison): Actual observed pedestrian behavior
- **O** (Outcome): Prediction accuracy (measured as match rate between simulated and observed flow patterns)

## Example 2: Urban Analysis

### Initial Question
> "How does urban morphology affect microclimate?"

### Diagnosis

**Problems identified:**

1. **Too broad**: Both "urban morphology" and "microclimate" cover many variables
2. **No population/setting**: Which cities? What climate zone?
3. **Unmeasurable as stated**: What aspect of microclimate?

### Refinement Process

**Q1:** "Which aspect of urban morphology interests you most?"

**A1:** "Street canyon proportions - the height-to-width ratio of streets."

**Updated question:**
> "How does street canyon geometry affect microclimate?"

---

**Q2:** "Which microclimate variable do you want to focus on?"

**A2:** "Thermal comfort for pedestrians, especially in summer."

**Updated question:**
> "How does street canyon geometry affect pedestrian thermal comfort in summer?"

---

**Q3:** "In what setting? Different climates will show different effects."

**A3:** "Mediterranean cities - hot, dry summers."

**Updated question:**
> "How does street canyon height-to-width ratio affect pedestrian thermal comfort during summer in Mediterranean cities?"

---

**Q4:** "How will you measure thermal comfort?"

**A4:** "Using PET - Physiological Equivalent Temperature. It's a standard index."

### Final Result

**Before:**
> "How does urban morphology affect microclimate?"

**After:**
> "What is the relationship between street canyon height-to-width ratio and pedestrian thermal comfort (PET) during summer months in Mediterranean climate cities?"

### Framework Validation (FINER)

- **F** (Feasible): Can be studied with simulation and/or field measurement
- **I** (Interesting): Relevant to urban design practice and climate adaptation
- **N** (Novel): While street canyons are studied, Mediterranean-specific guidance is limited
- **E** (Ethical): No human subjects concerns with physical measurements
- **R** (Relevant): Climate adaptation is a pressing urban planning concern

## Example 3: Architecture Research

### Initial Question
> "Is sustainable architecture better?"

### Diagnosis

**Problems identified:**

1. **Hidden assumption**: Assumes "better" is objective and universal
2. **Undefined terms**: "Sustainable architecture" means different things
3. **No measurable outcome**: "Better" by what measure?
4. **Missing population**: Better for whom?

### Refinement Process

**Q1:** "Better in what way? For whom?"

**A1:** "I'm interested in whether people actually prefer living in green buildings."

**Updated question:**
> "Do people prefer living in sustainable buildings?"

---

**Q2:** "What do you mean by 'sustainable buildings' - certified green buildings? Buildings with specific features?"

**A2:** "Buildings with visible green features like green roofs and natural ventilation - things people can see and experience."

**Updated question:**
> "Do residents prefer living in buildings with visible green features?"

---

**Q3:** "How would you measure preference? And which residents?"

**A3:** "Through satisfaction surveys. Looking at residential buildings in Germany."

**Updated question:**
> "How do visible green building features affect resident satisfaction in German multi-family housing?"

---

**Q4:** "Which specific features? 'Visible green features' is still broad."

**A4:** "Green facades and rooftop gardens specifically - these are the most visible."

### Final Result

**Before:**
> "Is sustainable architecture better?"

**After:**
> "How do green facades and rooftop gardens affect resident satisfaction in German multi-family housing?"

### Framework Validation (PICO)

- **P** (Population): Residents of German multi-family housing
- **I** (Intervention): Buildings with green facades and/or rooftop gardens
- **C** (Comparison): Similar buildings without these features
- **O** (Outcome): Resident satisfaction (measured via validated survey instrument)

## Example 4: Sustainability Studies

### Initial Question
> "How can we make cities more sustainable?"

### Diagnosis

**Problems identified:**

1. **Too broad**: "Cities" and "sustainable" are each vast topics
2. **Prescriptive framing**: Assumes we should, asks how - but research should establish what works first
3. **No specific outcome**: Sustainability has environmental, social, and economic dimensions
4. **Action-oriented, not research-oriented**: This is a policy goal, not a research question

### Refinement Process

**Q1:** "What aspect of urban sustainability interests you most?"

**A1:** "Reducing carbon emissions from buildings."

**Updated question:**
> "How can we reduce carbon emissions from urban buildings?"

---

**Q2:** "This is still quite broad and prescriptive. What specifically do you want to understand?"

**A2:** "I want to know if retrofitting old buildings is actually better than demolishing and rebuilding with modern standards."

**Updated question:**
> "Is retrofitting existing buildings more effective than demolition and new construction for reducing carbon emissions?"

---

**Q3:** "What type of buildings? And how would you measure 'more effective'?"

**A3:** "Pre-1970s residential buildings in European cities. Looking at lifecycle carbon emissions."

**Updated question:**
> "How do lifecycle carbon emissions compare between deep energy retrofit and demolition-plus-reconstruction for pre-1970s residential buildings in European cities?"

---

**Q4:** "Can you specify what 'deep energy retrofit' means for your study?"

**A4:** "Retrofit to Passive House standard - that's a well-defined target."

### Final Result

**Before:**
> "How can we make cities more sustainable?"

**After:**
> "How do lifecycle carbon emissions compare between Passive House retrofit and demolition-plus-reconstruction for pre-1970s multi-family residential buildings in temperate European climates?"

### Framework Validation (PICO)

- **P** (Population): Pre-1970s multi-family residential buildings in temperate Europe
- **I** (Intervention): Passive House standard retrofit
- **C** (Comparison): Demolition and new construction to current standards
- **O** (Outcome): Lifecycle carbon emissions (kgCO2e over building lifespan)

## Example 5: Participatory Design

### Initial Question
> "How can participatory design be improved?"

### Diagnosis

**Problems identified:**

1. **Prescriptive**: Assumes improvement is needed before establishing current state
2. **Vague outcome**: "Improved" is undefined
3. **Missing context**: Participatory design of what? With whom?

### Refinement Process

**Q1:** "What kind of participatory design, and what problems have you observed?"

**A1:** "Urban planning workshops. I've noticed that dominant voices often overshadow quieter participants."

**Updated question:**
> "How can participatory urban planning workshops achieve more balanced participation?"

---

**Q2:** "This is still prescriptive. Could we reframe to first understand the problem?"

**A2:** "Good point. I want to know what factors lead to unequal participation."

**Updated question:**
> "What factors influence participation equality in urban planning workshops?"

---

**Q3:** "How would you measure participation equality?"

**A3:** "Speaking time distribution and idea contribution tracking."

**Updated question:**
> "What workshop design factors affect the distribution of speaking time among participants in urban planning workshops?"

---

**Q4:** "Which specific factors? The full range would be a large study."

**A4:** "Mainly interested in comparing facilitation techniques - active facilitation vs. letting discussion flow naturally."

### Final Result

**Before:**
> "How can participatory design be improved?"

**After:**
> "How does structured facilitation compared to open discussion affect the distribution of speaking time among participants in urban planning workshops?"

### Framework Validation (PICO)

- **P** (Population): Participants in urban planning workshops
- **I** (Intervention): Structured facilitation techniques
- **C** (Comparison): Open (unstructured) discussion format
- **O** (Outcome): Distribution of speaking time (measured by standard deviation or Gini coefficient of individual contributions)

## Pattern Summary

| Initial Problem | Refinement Strategy |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Too broad | Ask "which aspect specifically?" |
| Undefined jargon | Ask "can you define X concretely?" |
| No measurement | Ask "how would you know?" |
| Missing population | Ask "who/what specifically?" |
| Missing setting | Ask "in what context?" |
| Prescriptive framing | Reframe as descriptive first |
| Bundled questions | Separate and prioritize |
| Hidden assumptions | Make explicit and test |
